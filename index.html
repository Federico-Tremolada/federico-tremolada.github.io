Featured Project

Enthesis FEM Model — Stress and Energy Distribution in Functionally Graded Interfaces

A finite element study of the tendon-to-bone interface (enthesis), developed to investigate how different material transition laws affect stress transfer and elastic energy distribution.

  • Problem: sharp material transitions generate stress concentrations and may reduce structural efficiency.
  • Method: 2D FEM models developed in Abaqus and compared across sharp, linear, exponential, and power-law material gradients.
  • Post-processing: Python and MATLAB workflows used to extract S11 profiles, von Mises stress, and elastic energy indicators.
  • Main result: functionally graded configurations reduced stress concentration compared with the sharp interface, with power-law solutions emerging as the most effective compromise.
